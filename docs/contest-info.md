## 3. The Challenge

Teams are invited to design **standalone AI translation devices powered entirely on-device using Edge AI**.

### Goal

Enable natural bilingual conversations with minimal latency, even in **noisy, hands-busy, or low-connectivity environments**.

### Solution Requirements

1. **Edge AI models** — On-device ML combining speech recognition, translation, and synthesis. No cloud dependency. Optimized for speed and accuracy.
2. **User-centered design** — Intuitive device interaction designed for workers in factories, construction sites, and logistics centers, with minimal training required.

> **Note:** Contestants are encouraged to use models from the [Qualcomm AI Hub](https://aihub.qualcomm.com/), which contains hundreds of models (ControlNet, Stable Diffusion, Llama) that have already been pre-optimized (quantized and compiled) to run on Snapdragon platforms.

### Technical Requirements

- Solutions may be deployed on **any portable device format**, without restriction to predefined device categories.
- Specific benchmark values are published **prior to the prototype submission phase** (see §4).
- The final round features **live demonstrations and winner announcement**, where shortlisted teams present their working prototypes to a panel of judges.

## 11. FAQ

**Source:** Extracted from the official contest page JS bundle (accordion content loaded dynamically).

### 1. Who can participate in the OneVoice AI Challenge?

The competition is open to participants worldwide, with a strong focus on innovators from Vietnam and across Asia — independent researchers, startups, small technology teams, and university teams. Teams of any size are welcome.

### 2. Do participants need hardware development experience?

Hardware experience is helpful but not strictly required. Teams may combine expertise in AI, software development, embedded systems, or product design. The key requirement is the ability to develop and demonstrate a functional translation prototype integrated with a device.

### 3. What types of devices can be used for the solution?

Participants may deploy their solution on any compact handheld or wearable device format, without restriction to specific device categories.

### 4. Can we submit an existing product or startup solution?

No — submissions must be original solutions developed specifically for this competition. Products that are already commercialized, revenue-generating, or currently in mass production are not eligible. However, early-stage prototypes or research concepts are allowed.

### 5. Is remote participation allowed?

Yes. Most development stages, including registration, proposal submission, and prototype development, can be completed remotely. However, finalist teams will be invited to present their solutions in person during the final round in Ho Chi Minh City.

### 6. What do finalists need to present in the final round?

Finalist teams must present:

- A working prototype or demo of their translation device
- A 5-minute demonstration video
- Technical documentation of the system architecture and AI model
- A pitch presentation explaining the solution and its potential applications
- A business case for commercialization, covering potential use cases, target market, and product development vision

### 7. Who owns the intellectual property (IP) of the solution?

Teams retain ownership of the intellectual property for the solutions they develop. However, participants must ensure that their submissions do not violate any third-party intellectual property rights. Additional IP guidelines will be shared with shortlisted teams.

### 8. When does registration open?

Registration for the OneVoice AI Challenge opens from May to June 2026. Teams submit their applications through the official registration form on the competition website (Luma).
