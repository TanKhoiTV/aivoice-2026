This is a product-engineering competition with academic evaluation rigor

The judges are looking for working prototypes with real measurements — not slides or theoretical models.

Qualcomm's DNA

Working on-device Snapdragon demos they can showcase. RTF < 1.0, sub-2s latency, no-cloud, field testing — all engineering gates.

Saigon AI Hub's DNA

Academically defensible winners. BLEU/COMET, MOS panel, proprietary benchmark dataset, published scoring criteria.

Strategy

Our edge is not invention — it's execution rigor on real hardware

The components are available to every team. What separates us is what we measure and engineer around them.

Form Factor

Phone-in-pocket beats wearables for sustained Edge AI

The "hands-free" concern

Contest says "hands-busy" environments — not a wearable requirement

No rule mandates hands-free device format

Phone on lanyard + VAD trigger + Bluetooth earpiece = hands remain free

Phone vs. wearable compute

Wearables: tiny battery, weak NPU, overheat under continuous AI load

Phone (SD8G2): 16GB RAM, dedicated NPU, thermal mass for hours of inference

Zero additional hardware cost in enterprise deployment
